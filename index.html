About

A New Kind of Teaching Lab at Said

The Centre for Pedagogical Innovation (CPi) is a School-wide initiative at Said Business School, University of Oxford. It transforms the HIVE - the School's immersive telepresence facility - into a dual-purpose Teaching Innovation Lab for generative AI and extended reality in education.

The CPi serves faculty, researchers, students, and teaching staff across Oxford. It provides a structured, low-risk environment to experiment with new teaching approaches, evaluate their effectiveness, and translate findings into publishable research and improved practice.

This is a genuinely collaborative initiative, co-designed with the School's Digital and Technology leadership, faculty champions, student representatives, and the University's AI and Machine Learning Competency Centre - with formal ties to the Centre for Teaching and Learning across the wider University.

Our Foundation

Built on the Oxford Tutorial Ethos

Oxford's tutorial system - personalised, mentor-led, intellectually demanding - is the most celebrated pedagogical model in the world. The CPi translates that ethos into the MBA and EMBA context, using AI and immersive technology to free instructors for richer human interaction, not to replace it.

Mission

Six Pillars of the CPi

The Centre operates across six interconnected areas, each designed to strengthen the others and produce lasting improvements in teaching quality at Oxford and beyond.

Pillar 01

Teaching Innovation Lab

A physical and virtual space for structured experimentation with generative AI and extended reality in pedagogy - a low-risk environment for faculty to try, evaluate, and refine new teaching approaches.

Pillar 02

AI Literacy for Educators

A structured professional development programme helping Oxford academics understand, critique, and confidently integrate AI into their teaching - grounded in pedagogy, not technology for its own sake.

Pillar 03

Pedagogical Research

A supported environment where new teaching methods are developed, rigorously evaluated, and translated into publishable scholarship - making the CPi a home for the Scholarship of Teaching and Learning at Oxford.

Pillar 04

University-wide Partnership

Formal ties with the Oxford Centre for Teaching and Learning, the Department of Education, and partner institutions globally - extending the CPi's reach across the collegiate University.

Pillar 05

Teaching Excellence

The home of Teaching at Said - recognising and systematically improving teaching quality through peer observation, faculty development, and the annual Teaching Excellence Awards.

Pillar 06

Assessment Redesign

Supporting faculty in designing assessments that work in an AI-present world - that genuinely test what they intend to test, align with Oxford policy, and develop the skills graduates need.

Programmes

What We Offer

The CPi delivers structured programmes for faculty, students, and academic staff across Said and the wider University.

Faculty Development

AI Literacy for Educators

A six-module blended programme for Oxford academic staff. Covers AI fundamentals, discipline-specific applications, pedagogical integration, assessment design, ethics, and hands-on toolkit building. Aligned with Oxford's AI assessment policy.

6 modules  |  Blended delivery  |  Oxford-wide  |  Launching Michaelmas 2026
MBA Programme

AI Fluency for MBA Leaders

A six-week module embedded in the Said MBA - making Said the first Oxford programme to formally require AI fluency for graduation. Covers AI strategy, data literacy, ethics, and an AI business case capstone.

6 weeks  |  MBA cohort  |  Said Business School  |  Launching Michaelmas 2026
Teaching Excellence

Master Teachers at Oxford

A termly seminar series where Oxford's finest educators demonstrate their practice publicly. One session per term, themed around the tutorial system, AI integration, and assessment design. Co-hosted with the Oxford Centre for Teaching and Learning.

3 sessions per year  |  Open to Oxford academics  |  First session Michaelmas 2026
Student Partnership

Student Consultants on Teaching

A structured programme pairing undergraduates with faculty members as pedagogical partners for one term. Students observe teaching sessions and provide structured feedback using the Oxford Student Consultant Protocol.

One term  |  Said pilot  |  Zero cost  |  Launching Trinity 2026
Peer Development

Peer Observation of Teaching

A formative, voluntary peer observation programme for Oxford academics. Cross-disciplinary pairs observe each other's teaching using the Oxford Peer Observation Framework - adapted for tutorials, seminars, and lectures.

One cycle per term  |  Cross-divisional  |  Developmental, not evaluative
Annual Conference

Oxford Forum on Teaching, Learning and AI

The UK's reference annual conference on AI and pedagogy in higher education. Co-chaired with the Oxford Centre for Teaching and Learning, hosted at Said. Open to practitioners from across UK and European higher education.

Annual  |  Said Business School  |  UK and European HE  |  Inaugural edition 2027
Research and Use Cases

Teaching Innovation in Practice

The CPi supports five active areas of pedagogical experimentation and research - each grounded in educational evidence and evaluated rigorously before broader rollout.

Use Case 01

AI-Enhanced Case Study Discussions

Generative AI as a pedagogical co-pilot: real-time scenario generation, case-specific dialogue tools, and discussion prompts - freeing class time for deeper human engagement.

Use Case 02

Immersive Business Simulations

VR-based role-plays and decision-making scenarios powered by generative AI - from boardroom crises to supply chain emergencies - in a safe, repeatable environment.

Use Case 03

AI-Assisted Decision Labs

Analytics labs where student teams use AI to parse complex datasets, generate hypotheses, and learn to critically evaluate AI outputs as a core management competency.

Use Case 04

Personalised Feedback at Scale

AI tutors and feedback tools integrated with Canvas - providing formative feedback on written work and assessment briefs while reducing faculty administrative burden.

Use Case 05

Hybrid and Global Learning

Mixed-reality connectivity that extends Oxford's physical classroom globally - using the HIVE's screen infrastructure alongside updated AI-driven engagement tools.

SoTL

Scholarship of Teaching and Learning

A formal SoTL programme supporting Oxford faculty to conduct rigorous research on their teaching practice and publish findings - with seed grants and a CPi-convened working group.

Active Research Partnership

The CPi is developing a cross-site comparative research programme on AI and pedagogy across diverse global contexts, in collaboration with the Digital Futures Institute at Teachers College, Columbia University, and partner institutions in the Global South. This work is grounded in a Fulbright scholarship and published in AI and Society (2024).
